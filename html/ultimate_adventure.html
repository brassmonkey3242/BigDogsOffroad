BIG DOGS ULTIMATE ADVENTURE (BDUA)

Starting summer 2013

INTRODUCTION

The Big Dog Ultimate Adventure (BDUA) is a road trip through western Virginia, West Virginia, western Maryland and western Pennsylvania.  (BDUA) is a combination of two of Big Dogs Pack It In (PII) trips, north and south.  Normally each PII, north or south, is 3 days in duration over a holiday weekend.  For example, Labor Day weekend, Labor Day is always the first Monday in September.  BDUA will be 4 days in duration, Fri, Sat, Sun & Labor Day.     

For BDUA we will need to take at least one vacation day, Friday, to make the four day BDUA.  Do not miss Friday.  Friday has the most beautiful sight on the entire east coast.   

The Big Dog himself will not drive his Jeep, rather he will drive a Chevy Duramax Diesel dually as a tow vehicle and tow an empty one-car trailer in case anyone breaks down badly and needs a tow back to civilization.  Can store gear in the bed of his truck. 

This BDUA will be entirely on the highway.  No off-roading.  Lots of sight-seeing which include 12 beautiful landmarks.  No need to go to Colorado or Europe!  I have found many people don’t know what is in “their backyard!”  After this trip, you will know.  The Big Dog will also teach some of the Survival Training course at night around the campfire for free.         

If you can’t make Friday day and want to join up with us Friday night or Saturday morning, call or email me ahead of the BDUA and I will tell you where to meet us Friday night or Saturday morning and at what time. 

If you can’t stay with us through Monday, you can always leave on Sunday at any time. 

Everyone should bring maps of Virginia, West Virginia, Maryland and Pennsylvania.  I’ll have additional maps for you too.  Bring cameras, binoculars and bathing suit.  

You must PRE-REGISTER, make RESERVATIONS and PAY Big Dogs for this Ultimate Adventure  BEFORE the end of May.  People all over the east coast are reserving these locations and we must beat them.  I will make the reservations upon your payment to Big Dogs.      

SCHEDULE

Friday

Meet in Franklin West Virginia at the Fireside Cafe, formerly known as Thompson’s Restaurant, next to the Exxon station at 10 AM.  Leave at 11 AM.  Breakfast at 10 AM.   

#1.  11 AM we will depart to see the most beautiful sight on the entire east coast.  This vista rival’s the view of the Grand Canyon from the south rim.  Need I say more.   

#2.  Drive to the highest point in West Virginia and see a 360 degree vista.  Note, there is an asphalt road all the way to the top!  Upon arrival, climb the 2 story stone tower for better views.         

#3.  Get an overview of our Allegheny Mountains weekend with a visit to a million dollar mock up of all terrain, trails, back roads and highways.  The forests of the Central Appalachians are filled with wilderness areas that consist of meadows, sphagnum bogs, dense forests, tundra like plateau’s and wind beaten cliffs.  This facility is open only from 9 AM to 4:30 PM.     

#4.  We will do a 30-45 minute foot-walking climb to the top of a spectacular rock formation.  This rock formation goes straight up for 1,200 feet.  Professional climbers are on this formation every day.  Bring binoculars if you have them.   

#5.  Experience and explore the wilderness of West Virginia Sods.  This wilderness has many colored plants and bushes.  Scenic views and castle rocks to walk on. 

#6.  We will visit a gift shop that even the guys like!  It is a 2 story structure that looks like a ski resort.  Open 9 to 5 PM.  Also, several commercial caverns are available to explore.   

By late afternoon, we’ll go to the campground where we are staying for one night, Friday night.  Tent camping like at Big Dogs.  If you want a cabin or hotel, tell me ahead of time of the BDUA.  We have them, but need to make reservations early in the summer.  Hot showers available at the campground.  The Big Dog will also teach some of the Survival Training course around the campfire for free.     

Saturday

#7.  9 AM sharp leave for the 12 zip lines and 2 sky bridges that go down a mountain and through the trees.  This event goes to 12 AM noon.  

Lunch at nearby restaurant on the corner of road that goes to the highest point in West Virginia. 

#8.  See what fallen hemlock and red spruce needles can do to a 150 foot waterfall.  The surrounding rocks and vegetation is spectacular!          

#9.  Climb an abandoned cement fire tower.  The views from here are better than the highest point in West Virginia.  This abandoned fire tower is secluded.  You will never find it on your own!   

#10. Cross a bridge and on to the Blackwater 100 road, do a short hike along a rugged river carved canyon.  The solitude is stunning.     

Camp on a fresh water lake, the first of two nights.  Tent camping like at Big Dogs.  If you want a cabin or hotel, tell me ahead of time of the BDUA.  We have them. 

Night time entertainment at the Honi Honi Bar or other restaurants Saturday night.              

Sunday

#11. Play at a water park.  Lots of activities.  

#12. Afternoon, water ski and speed boating for 3 hours, sight-see this huge lake from the lake itself! 

Camp on the fresh water lake, second of two nights.  This federal campground has a beach right on the wake.          

Monday

#13. White water rafting.  This company offers great photos of your group on the river.    Everybody purchases them because they are so good.  The decision to purchase can be made at the river. 

#14. Frank Lloyd Wright’s “Falling Waters”  This is for the women!  Mr. Wright built a home of stone on top of a stream.  Major tourist attraction.   

Plan for home.  This will be at 1 or 2 PM Monday afternoon.  This leaves plenty of time to return home no matter where you live.  Use Cumberland, Maryland as a final travel departing point.  On the way back from Keyser’s Ridge, stop at Grantsville Exit 19 for dinner at Penn Alps.  North on access road from I-68 to Rt 40, turn right for 2 miles.  Penn Alps is on your left.
